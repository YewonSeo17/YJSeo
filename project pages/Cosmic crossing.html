Cosmic crossing

Sep 15, 2023

Disabled individuals, especially those who use wheelchairs, are particularly vulnerable to fires. When a building is set on fire, people inside must immediately evacuate the building using the stairs. However, disabled individuals, the elderly, and children who are unable to navigate the path on their own often encounter difficulties while trying to escape. Additionally, in emergency situations, elevators or escalators cannot be used. Research shows that elderly individuals and people with disabilities are less likely to survive in such disastrous situations.

To address this problem, my co-workers and I have designed a structure called the "Underwater Walk". This structure provides an alternative means of escape from the building without relying on stairs or elevators. The structure is connected to each floor of the building, and long ramps offer people an additional route to safety during disasters. These ramps can also be used as walking paths, and LED signage displays will enhance the experience of walking through them.
